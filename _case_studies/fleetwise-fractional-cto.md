## The Challenge

Fleetwise had built a compelling route-optimisation product on a scrappy monolithic architecture. It worked brilliantly at SME scale. But with two enterprise pilots on the horizon — each requiring SOC 2 compliance, SSO integration, and sub-100ms API response times — the engineering team knew the existing system would not survive contact with the real workload.

The founding CTO was brilliant at product thinking but hadn't led a team through a technical transformation at this scale before. They needed an experienced hand alongside them, not a consultancy report.

## What We Did

**Technical audit.** We spent the first three weeks conducting a thorough audit of the existing codebase, infrastructure, and engineering practices. We identified four critical bottlenecks and three architectural decisions that were quietly accumulating future costs.

**Architecture roadmap.** Working with the in-house team, we designed a phased migration from the monolith to a service-oriented architecture — scoped deliberately to be achievable without halting product development. No big-bang rewrites.

**Hiring & team structure.** We defined three new roles, wrote the job descriptions, sat on interview panels, and helped the founders make the two most important engineering hires of the company's life — a senior backend engineer and a platform/SRE specialist.

**Compliance readiness.** We led the SOC 2 Type I readiness programme, coordinating with a specialist auditor and ensuring the engineering team had the processes and tooling in place to pass without disruption.

> "Having someone who had been through this before — who knew which shortcuts would cost us later and which were genuinely fine — was worth more than any amount of documentation."
>
> — CTO, Fleetwise

## The Outcome

Both enterprise pilots launched on schedule. Fleetwise passed their SOC 2 Type I audit on the first attempt. Average API response time dropped from 340ms to 68ms across the core endpoints.

The engagement ran for nine months. Azimuth holds a small equity stake and an ongoing board observer role.

## Key Takeaways

- Architecture decisions made at Series A often determine what is possible at Series B — get them right the first time
- Phased migrations almost always outperform big-bang rewrites in a product company under commercial pressure
- The two or three key engineering hires at scale-up stage have a disproportionate impact on long-term outcomes
